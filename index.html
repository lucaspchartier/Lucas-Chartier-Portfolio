About Me

My journey into web development.

  • May 2017

    Graduated from Lasell College

    I received my Bachelor of Science in entrepeneurship while working full-time at Lasell's on-campus college radio station, 102.9FM WLAS, located in Newton, Massachusetts. During my senior year, I was promoted to station manager in September of 2016.

  • June 2018

    Finished Digital Marketing Circuit course at General Assembly

    A year after receiving my bachelor's degree from Lasell, I started looking into General Assembly for courses as I continued to work at my gym and ultimately chose to take their 5-week online course in digital marketing in May of 2018.

  • Summer 2018

    Began teaching myself basic coding

    As I gradually opened up to General Assembly, I decided that it was time to make a change in my career, and wanted to further pursue web development. I began using online resources like Codecademy to gradually teach myself HTML, CSS, and JavaScript.

  • January 2019

    Finished Web Development Immersive course at General Assembly's Boston campus

    When the opportunity came, I reached out to General Assembly again, and enrolled in their 3-month web development immersive program in October of 2018. During the course, I constructed 4 full-stack web applications as I started to look into opportunities in web development.

Travel Blog

A full-stack application for users to log and store their hotel stays during their travels.

For my fourth project, this was my final capstone project, and I decided to mainly focus on styling, layout and UI. This full-stack application was constructed using Ruby on Rails for the API and ReactJS for the client. Because my main focus was on the UI, the application itself appears more appealing to travel enthusiasts with the travel-themed layout.

Life and Dreams

A full-stack application for users to add goals they want to achieve in their lifetime and check them off upon completion.

For my third project, this was my first time collaborating with two other developers in this group project. This full-stack application was constructed using Express for the API and HTML, CSS, JavaScript, and jQuery for the client. Over the course of this project, we had to logistically plan out which developer was working on which part of the API and client, and we also had to compare and merge various pull requests on GitHub.
